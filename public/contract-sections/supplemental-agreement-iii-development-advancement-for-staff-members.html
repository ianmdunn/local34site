Supplemental Agreement III

Development & Advancement for Staff Members

1. The University, in conjunction with Local 34, will create a Career Development Office for Staff Members.

  1. The Office will be established within six (6) months of ratification of this agreement and accountable to the joint Union and University Best Practices Policy Board.

  2. The Office will be led by a University manager. The Union will participate in the selection process for this position.

  3. The Office will oversee:

1. The Human Resources Clerical & Technical Staffing Representatives;

2. Yale Temporary Staffing Services;

3. Support to laid-off Clerical & Technical Staff Members;

4. Counseling support to assist Clerical & Technical Staff Members seeking advancement or a transition to a different job family; and

5. Career development seminars, resume writing workshops, coaching on interviewing, etc.

  1. The Office will work on its initiatives with the Job Search Team, which shall include three (3) Union members released by the University. The University will participate in the selection of those Union members assigned to the Job Search Team. \3

  2. The Office will track internal advancement success versus targets established.

1. Development and Advancement Councils (DAC), as further described below, will be established for five (5) major job families (Administrative Assistants, Research Assistants, Financial Assistants, Account Assistants, and Library Staff) that Make Up More Than 70% of Local 34 Membership and Nearly 66% of the Annual C&T Position Openings.

  1. The Councils will be made up of University representatives and Union members with experience and expertise in the respective job families.

  2. The Councils’ charter is to expand internal promotions in their respective job families.

  3. The Councils will:

1. Recommend technical and soft skill training curriculum and identify training capabilities and roles, to overcome current barriers to internal advancement. Staff Members can make recommendations for improving training to the Development and Advancement Councils.

2. Support, mentor, and advocate for C&Ts seeking advancement or alternative assignments in their job family.

3. Assist IEP candidates within their respective job family to gain temporary and/or permanent placement.

4. Recommend how to utilize local hiring to build a pipeline to YTSS and Yale job openings in their respective job family.

5. Coordinate their activities with the Career Development Office and report regularly to the joint Union and University Policy Board.

6. May recommend training and advancement programs to the Policy Board for action and resources.

  1. An Administrative Assistant DAC will be established and operational within six (6) months following ratification of this agreement.

  2. Union members will be released on work time to conduct the business of the DACs (release time budget to be agreed upon by Union and University).

2. The University and Local 34 will expand opportunities for qualified bidders for promotional positions as described below:

  1. The University and Local 34 will discuss ways to strengthen the process by which qualification and selection determinations are made, in accordance with contract language, and make improvements as necessary. Specifically, they will:

1. Document current application and qualifications process.

2. Jointly develop feedback checklists for internal candidates to give them a clearer picture of why they weren’t successful candidates.

3. Jointly recommend and implement ways to communicate improved qualifications and selection processes to staff and hiring managers.

4. Local 34 will periodically review qualifications process and checklists.

5. Jointly agreed upon method to address and quickly resolve questions of individual qualifications for jobs.

6. Jointly continue to work to improve qualification processes.

  1. Hiring supervisors may elect to relax the posted requirements for a particular opening in order to hire an internal or laid-off Staff Member.\4

  2. All qualified bidders will be interviewed either by Human Resources, the Hiring Manager, or both. Hiring Managers retain the right to interview qualified candidates.

  3. Local 34 will receive a weekly electronic copy all Local 34 job postings.

  4. Where there are three (3) internal qualified bidders in the five job families with Development and Advancement Councils, forty-eight (48) hours will be given to the Union to review the reasons for selecting an external candidate. If needed, additional information will be requested from the manager by Human Resources.

  5. Provide a voluntary forty-five (45) day trial period for internal candidates bidding on promotional positions to encourage managers to try them in the position;

1. either party may opt-in for the trial;

2. either manager or Staff Member can elect not to proceed within the forty-five (45) day window;

3. Staff Member can return to prior position at prior hourly rate;

4. Staff Member can only opt out of one (1) position during a period of one (1) year; manager can only opt out once per position;

5. the University will monitor the progress of a trial period and share progress reports with the Union.

6. The Union and University will review how well this process is working on an ongoing basis and make adjustments as necessary.

3. Metric:

  1. Between January 20, 2013 and July 1, 2014, the University will fill internally more than sixty-six percent (66%) of D & E positions with three (3) or more qualified internal applicants in the five (5) job families covered by Development and Advancement Councils. Every month, the University will share with the union a report of all filled bargaining unit positions.  It will also provide a report of all jobs filled to which three (3) or more qualified internals applied. These reports will be designed jointly.

  2. During the life of this contract, if this measure is not met, or if the University fails to maintain this progress over any six (6) month period after July 1, 2014, departments will be offered an incentive package to hire internal candidates.  The incentive package will be the cost of salary and benefits during a forty-five (45) day trial period (subject to managers’ agreement to opt in).

4. Streamline the Job Audit Process to Make it Less Cumbersome and Work Intensive.

  1. Utilize new job audit form created by joint Union and University project team.

  2. Redefine the criteria for E Level positions to more broadly use this level to recognize Staff Members with specialized skills and qualifications.

5. In order to assure there is no slippage on the appropriate classification of Clerical & Technical positions:

  1. The University and Local 34 will engage in an ongoing review and resolution of questions of inclusion or exclusion of positions in the bargaining unit.

  2. Local 34 will be advised of new and replacement M&P job postings, and job titles added, in Levels 20-23 and will be provided job content background as requested.

  3. Local 34 will be notified of any Clerical & Technical position being considered for upgrading to an M&P position in advance of final decision. \5

6. The University will introduce a Performance Appraisal Process for Clerical & Technical Staff to improve on-the-job feedback and skills development.\6

  1. Appraisal utilized to be the form jointly developed by the Union and University project or the “FOCUS” form if already in place.

  2. Copies of appraisals may be released to the hiring manager if the Staff Member agrees. There will be a place on the form for the Staff Member to elect this choice.

  3. A joint Union and University committee will monitor the Performance Appraisal introduction and resolve implementation issues if, and as, they occur.

  4. The Appraisal form will not be used as part of the progressive discipline process.

7. For job families without Development and Advancement Councils, the University will create a Central Development Budget to fund training initiatives.

  1. The Budget will initially be set at fifty-thousand dollars ($50,000) and the University will reassess as needed if requests exceed this budget.

  2. Staff Members, working with their managers, can apply for funding for their training proposals.

1. The Career Development Office and The Learning Center will facilitate and support training processes created.
